## Teaching Philosophy

I believe strong technical teaching connects fundamentals with working systems. My approach emphasizes project-based learning, reproducible coding habits, debugging confidence, and the link between theory and implementation. In research mentorship, I guide students through the complete research lifecycle: literature review, problem formulation, implementation, evaluation, writing, and presentation.

## Undergraduate Teaching

### North South University

**Teaching Assistant -- Operating System Design (CSE323)**  
*Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering*

- Taught operating system concepts including process scheduling, synchronization, memory management, file systems, and device I/O.
- Integrated operating system security, access control, virtualization, and containerization discussions into lab instruction.
- Mentored students through term projects focused on simulation and evaluation of operating system components.

*Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering*

## Research Mentorship & Instruction

- Mentored **4 undergraduate quantum researchers** through QKD protocol implementation, entanglement-based communication systems, and publication-oriented workflows.
- Supported research teams in translating prototypes into manuscripts through experiment design, analysis, technical writing, and revision.
- Helped students connect quantum computing concepts with deployable security applications.
