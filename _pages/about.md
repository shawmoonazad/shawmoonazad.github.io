> **I build quantum algorithms, hybrid quantum-classical systems, and quantum-safe security frameworks for practical deployment.**

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at **Cleveland State University** and a Graduate Research Assistant in the **Intelligent Secure Cyber-Systems and Applications Research Lab (ISCAR Lab)**. My current work focuses on quantum algorithms, post-quantum cryptography, quantum machine learning, and secure cloud communication.

At Cleveland State, I work on an NSF-funded research project on hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for secure cloud communication systems. I design reinforcement learning agents for protocol selection, optimize parameterized quantum circuits for NISQ-era hardware, and study how quantum and post-quantum security methods can be deployed under realistic latency, hardware, and threat-model constraints.

Previously, I was a Quantum Research Assistant at the **Optics-Quantum & AI Lab** at North South University, supervised by **Dr. Mahdy Rahman Chowdhury**, where I implemented QKD protocols such as BB84, B92, and E91 in IBM Qiskit and built hybrid quantum-classical security systems for image, audio, and cloud communication. I also worked with **Dr. Sifat Momen** on applied machine learning, explainable AI, and healthcare analytics.

My work has appeared in journals including **Journal of Information Security and Applications**, **IET Quantum Communication**, **Alexandria Engineering Journal**, **Engineering Science and Technology**, **Array**, and **PLOS ONE**. I am open to research collaborations and Summer 2027 quantum/AI research internship conversations.
