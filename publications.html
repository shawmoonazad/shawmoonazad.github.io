Quantum Secure Image Transmission: A Chaos-Assisted QKD Approach Using Entanglement

M. S. Azad*, R. Rayhan*, et al. • Equal Contribution 1st Author

IET Quantum Communication (Q2), 2025

This study proposes a secure image transmission framework that merges the E91 quantum key distribution protocol with logistic chaotic maps. By combining quantum entanglement for key exchange and chaos-based encryption for data protection, the system enhances resistance to quantum attacks. Simulation analyses show strong security metrics and efficient performance, making it a promising approach for quantum-era cybersecurity across sensitive sectors.

Multi-layered Security: Integrating QKD with Classical Cryptography to Enhance Steganographic Security

A. Sykot, M. S. Azad, et al. • 2nd Co-author

Alexandria Engineering Journal (Q1), 2025

This study develops a secure image encryption system that combines Quantum Key Distribution (E91 protocol) with AES encryption, SHA hashing, and deep learning-based steganography. Quantum-generated keys strengthen encryption security, while steganography conceals encrypted images for covert transmission. The hybrid quantum–classical framework enhances confidentiality, integrity, and resistance to eavesdropping, offering a practical step toward next-generation secure image communication.

QSAC: Quantum-assisted Secure Audio Communication using Entanglement, Audio Steganography, and Classical Encryption

M. S. Azad, et al. • Co-supervised

Engineering Science & Technology (Q1), 2025

This research proposes a secure audio transmission framework that integrates Quantum Key Distribution (E91 protocol) with the ChaCha20-Poly1305 encryption–authentication scheme and steganography. Quantum-generated keys provide eavesdropping resistance, while classical encryption ensures integrity and confidentiality. Experimental results confirm high randomness, strong resistance to quantum and classical attacks, and reliable message integrity, establishing a robust hybrid system for secure end-to-end audio communication.

SAD: Self-Assessment of Depression for Bangladeshi University Students Using ML and NLP

M. S. Azad, et al. • First Author

ARRAY (Q1), 2024

This research analyzes depression among Bangladeshi university students by linking social factors with multiple machine learning, NLP, and explainable AI techniques. Support Vector Machines achieved 99.14% accuracy using the PHQ-9 scale, while the stacking ensemble model performed best across social dimensions. BioBERT led NLP-based methods with 90.34% accuracy, and LIME improved model interpretability. Overall, traditional ML models outperformed large language models for structured depression assessment data

Predictive Modeling of Consumer Purchase Behavior: TPB + Machine Learning for Actionable Insights

M. S. Azad, et al. • First Author

PLOS ONE (Q1), 2023

This study integrates the Theory of Planned Behavior (attitude, social norm, and perceived behavioral control) with machine learning to predict online consumer purchasing behavior. Among eight tested algorithms, gradient boosting achieved the best accuracy (0.91) and F1 score (0.91). Using Explainable AI via LIME, the model’s decision-making process was made interpretable, offering actionable insights for businesses to tailor marketing strategies based on behavioral determinants.
