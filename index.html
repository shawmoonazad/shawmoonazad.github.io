My Research Works in Brief

Quantum Computing Research

[1] Enhancing the security of image transmission in the Quantum era: A Chaos-Assisted QKD Approach using entanglement.

TL;DR:

Methodology: Combined the E91 quantum key distribution (QKD) protocol with logistic chaotic map encryption to develop a secure image transmission system. Quantum entanglement and CHSH inequality were used for eavesdropping detection, while chaotic maps added classical encryption strength.

Outcome: Simulation results showed strong encryption performance through metrics like keyspace/sensitivity, entropy, histogram uniformity, execution time, and differential attack resistance. The scheme proved efficient for real-time, secure image communication.

Impact: Provides a robust hybrid quantum-classical encryption framework addressing future threats posed by quantum computers, with potential applications in healthcare, defense, and finance for secure data communication.

[2] Multi-Layered Security System: Integrating Quantum Key Distribution with Classical Cryptography to Enhance Steganographic Security.

TL;DR:

Methodology: Developed a hybrid cryptographic system by integrating the E91 Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) protocol with SHA hashing and AES encryption to secure steganographic images. The quantum key ensures eavesdropping resistance, while SHA produces a high-entropy key for AES-based symmetric encryption. Steganography is used to hide critical data within images, adding a layer of obscurity.

Outcome: Experimental results on images (64×64 to 512×512 pixels) showed high entropy (avg. 7.9929), strong differential attack resistance (NPCR ≈ 99.69%, UACI ≈ 56.15%), and efficient performance (key generation in 5.78 s, encryption in 0.00149 s, decryption in 0.00175 s).

Impact: Provides a quantum-secure, fast, and reliable encryption framework for steganographic images, offering strong protection against both classical and future quantum cyber threats.

Machine Learning Research

[1] SAD: Self-assessment of depression for Bangladeshi university students using machine learning and NLP.

TL;DR:

Methodology: Analyzed depression among 520 Bangladeshi university students using eight depression assessment scales, traditional machine learning (ML) models, BERT-based NLP models, and generative large language models. Social factors such as relationships, parental pressure, academic satisfaction, and exposure to violence were incorporated. Explainable AI techniques (like LIME) were used to interpret model decisions.

Outcome: Support Vector Machines achieved 99.14% accuracy with the PHQ-9 scale. For social factor analysis, a stacking ensemble ML model reached 97.88% accuracy. Among NLP models, BioBERT performed best with 90.34% accuracy. Tree of Thought prompting with Claude Sonnet was the top performer among generative models, achieving 75.00% accuracy.

Impact: Demonstrates a highly effective and interpretable ML-driven framework for diagnosing depression, highlighting the role of social factors. This approach enhances early mental health assessment in academic settings, especially in resource-limited regions like Bangladesh.

Research Methodology:

[2] Predictive Modeling of Consumer Purchase Behavior on Social Media.

TL;DR:

Methodology: Integrated the Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)—comprising attitude, social norm, and perceived behavioral control—with eight machine learning algorithms (e.g., Gradient Boosting, SVM, Random Forest) to predict consumer online purchasing behavior. Explainable AI (LIME) was used to interpret model predictions.

Outcome: Gradient Boosting outperformed other models with 91% accuracy and a macro F1-score of 0.91 when all TPB factors were considered. LIME successfully interpreted predictions, showing high confidence in understanding behavioral influences.

Impact: Presents a novel, interpretable ML framework for analyzing online consumer behavior. Offers actionable insights for businesses to tailor marketing strategies based on psychological drivers, improving targeting and engagement through data-informed decisions.

Undergraduate-Directed Research (Thesis)

[1]ItihashQA: A Conversational AI-Based Question Answering System for Bangladeshi History.

Supervisor: Dr. Sifat Momen   Google Scholar

TL;DR:

Methodology: Developed ItihashQA, a domain-specific conversational retrieval-based question-answering system focused on Bangladeshi history. It combines the open-source Stable Vicuna LLM with expert-verified historical datasets to ensure accurate, context-aware responses. The system was evaluated against models like ChatGPT, Vicuna, and Koala using human-annotated ratings on a Likert scale.

Outcome: ItihashQA consistently outperformed other models across all question complexity levels, achieving high ratings for accuracy and contextual relevance. The approach avoided costly fine-tuning by leveraging retrieval augmentation and domain-specific data.

Impact: Presents a scalable, open-source alternative for reliable historical QA tasks. ItihashQA addresses LLM limitations like hallucination and misinformation, especially in resource-limited domains, and holds potential for educational, archival, and research applications.
