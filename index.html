Quantum systems researcher

Md Shawmoon Azad

PhD Student in Computer Science | Cleveland State University

I build hybrid quantum-classical systems for secure cloud communication, quantum-safe infrastructure, and efficient quantum AI. My work sits at the intersection of quantum algorithms, post-quantum cryptography, quantum machine learning, and deployable security systems.

I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the Intelligent Secure Cyber-Systems and Applications Research Lab (ISCAR Lab) at Cleveland State University. My current research is part of an NSF-funded project on hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for secure cloud communication, with emphasis on reinforcement learning for protocol selection, parameterized quantum circuits, NISQ-aware optimization, and performance evaluation.

Previously, I worked as a Quantum Research Assistant at the Optics-Quantum and AI Lab at North South University, where I implemented QKD protocols in IBM Qiskit, built quantum-secured image and audio communication systems, and mentored undergraduate researchers. I also worked on applied machine learning, explainable AI, and healthcare analytics with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at North South University.

I am open to research collaborations and Summer 2027 quantum/AI research internship conversations.
